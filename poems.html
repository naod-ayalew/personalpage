Why pretend? Why disguise?
You are not more pleasing to Him
even if you cover the dirtiness. Take no shame
from being a little selfish. You see,

Everybody is swimming towards what he wants.
He cannot even see it from land, yet
there it is, the will to search it.

Who wants to remain on the beach asking the wind,
How? How? How?
You need this so.
Swim, or sink trying; it is worthy.

They do not fly tonight to tell your story,
son with the signs of the war, or on the mangled
ground to stir up a frenzied rush
as the engines’ warning sounds across fields

They want just to remind you in your home,
where no spirits sneak through doors kept shut by paranoia,
of their own now empty through your acts,
you who feels more of Death than the dead,

for Lust, spinning their motors
eternal, fills them with envy
as you wring the sheets with living hands &

a gift no ghost from any time can break,
muted and sombered and sobered by their weakness,
they fly on, hoping to disappear when you do.

This van only goes so fast.
I am stuck in purgatory,
halfway between cities.
Two hours of sleep and
caffeine. I am awake.
Dreams left unfinished
ghost across the road.

We pass the proving grounds.
Ordinance was sprayed there,
across fields at no particular target,
the metal sins of soldiers buried.
If you close your eyes,
you will miss the women marching
in the ditch on the side of the road,
camouflaged imprints of
abuse stuck in time,
remnants of broken spirits
following every order. See
through them, there’s no time to feel.
The armories are fourteen years gone
along with the town’s significance.
Fog in the morning sets in and
disperses these visions
as fields and barns fade
anonymously into my rearview.

How many shelters are there still, and in these shelters,
how many doors, welcoming you even.
How many windows
through which one can see your life and works.

How many winged seeds from the future
swept up by the coming tempest,
on a soft day of celebration
will bloom and become yours.

How many lives stuck together;
but by the flight that your own life takes
in and of this world,
what great nothingness you will avoid.

Translated from Rainier Maria Rilke

Snow-covered earth where footprints are quickly pressed,
trees, bare, enclose us halfway from nightfall. It’s
easy to want in black and white, such
evenings crafted soundly, but these

proud lights in fashion throw siren songs
East-West in waves for capturing travelers
cold. We want to touch this source of
alien energy lifting, sparking

each thoughtful man’s designed curiosity.
Static undone by prisms of molecules
breaks green and spreads a cloth, our homes are
afterthoughts that we cannot bring fire to.

I.

This is the blue I imagine seawater to be,
the same blue in a newborn’s nursery,
Heaven’s blue walls in my own room.
I sleep here, sometimes accompanied by
a stranger. No fresco of guardian angels
smiling above me rosy-cheeked, but I do see
Jacob’s ladder & its infinite exhaustion with
no end in sight, like swimming towards a horizon’s
black divide. I wish more than anything to sleep
tonight
