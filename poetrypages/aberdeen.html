N.Y. The Gleam Pilots Aberdeen to Philadelphia Portrait Intérieur On High

This van only goes so fast.
I am stuck in purgatory,
halfway between cities.
Two hours of sleep and
caffeine. I am awake.
Dreams left unfinished
ghost across the road.

We pass the proving grounds.
Ordinance was sprayed there,
across fields at no particular target,
the metal sins of soldiers buried.
If you close your eyes,
you will miss the women marching
in the ditch on the side of the road,
camouflaged imprints of
abuse stuck in time,
remnants of broken spirits
following every order. See
through them, there’s no time to feel.
The armories are fourteen years gone
along with the town’s significance.
Fog in the morning sets in and
disperses these visions
as fields and barns fade
anonymously into my rearview.
